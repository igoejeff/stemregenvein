How VENCLOSE™ RFA Works

VENCLOSE Radiofrequency Ablation uses precisely controlled heat energy to collapse and seal diseased varicose veins from the inside. Blood is naturally rerouted to healthy veins, and the treated vein is gradually absorbed by the body.

1

Ultrasound Mapping

Your specialist maps the diseased vein network using duplex ultrasound to plan the most effective treatment path.

2

Local Anesthesia

Tumescent local anesthetic is applied along the vein. You remain fully awake and comfortable throughout the procedure.

3

Catheter Placement

A thin VENCLOSE catheter is guided into the diseased vein under ultrasound visualization through a tiny access point.

4

Radiofrequency Energy

Controlled RF energy heats and seals the vein wall as the catheter is slowly withdrawn, closing the entire segment.

5

Walk Out

You put on compression stockings and walk out of the office. Most patients resume light activities the same day.

VENCLOSE™ RFA FAQs

What is VENCLOSE RFA and how is it different from surgery?

Unlike traditional vein stripping surgery, VENCLOSE RFA requires no incisions, no general anesthesia, and no hospital stay. It is performed in our office with local anesthesia in under an hour.

Is VENCLOSE RFA painful?

Most patients feel only mild pressure or warmth during the procedure. Local anesthesia ensures comfort throughout. Post-procedure soreness is typically mild and resolves within a few days.

How soon will I see results after VENCLOSE RFA?

Symptom relief (aching, heaviness, swelling) is often felt within days. The treated veins gradually disappear over 8–12 weeks as they are absorbed by the body.

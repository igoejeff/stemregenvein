What Are Spider Veins?

Spider veins (telangiectasias) are small, dilated blood vessels that appear close to the skin surface. They often look like a web, starburst, or branching pattern in shades of red, blue, or purple — most commonly on the legs and face.

While usually harmless, spider veins can cause burning, itching, or aching — and many patients seek treatment to restore their confidence and comfort.

Signs You May Have Spider Veins

  • Red, blue, or purple web-like veins on legs or face
  • Burning or itching sensation over affected area
  • Mild aching after prolonged standing or sitting
  • Self-consciousness about vein appearance
  • Worsening symptoms during pregnancy or with age

Spider Vein FAQs

How many sclerotherapy sessions will I need?

Most patients require 2–4 sessions spaced 4–6 weeks apart. The number depends on the extent of spider veins and your individual response to treatment.

When will my spider veins disappear?

After sclerotherapy, spider veins typically fade over 4–8 weeks as the body naturally absorbs the treated vessels. Laser treatment results may appear slightly sooner.

Can spider veins come back after treatment?

Treated veins do not return. However, new spider veins may develop over time due to genetics, hormones, or lifestyle factors. Maintenance sessions can address any new veins that appear.

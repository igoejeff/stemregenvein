What Are Varicose Veins?

Varicose veins are twisted, enlarged veins that appear just beneath the skin — most commonly in the legs. They occur when the valves inside the veins weaken, causing blood to pool instead of flowing efficiently back to the heart. Left untreated, varicose veins can progress from a cosmetic nuisance to a painful medical condition.

At Stem Regen Medical, our board-certified specialists have treated over 5,000 patients with advanced, minimally invasive techniques that are covered by most major insurance plans.

Common Symptoms

  • Bulging, rope-like veins visible on the legs
  • Aching, heavy, or throbbing legs — especially after standing
  • Leg swelling that worsens throughout the day
  • Itching, burning, or restless legs at night
  • Skin discoloration or hardening near the ankles
  • Cramping or fatigue in the calves

Gold Standard

VENCLOSE™ RFA

Radiofrequency thermal energy seals the diseased vein from the inside. 30–45 min in-office. Walk out the same day.

Varithena® Microfoam

Polidocanol foam injected to chemically close and collapse varicose veins. Excellent for tortuous veins.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is varicose vein treatment covered by insurance?

Yes. When vein disease is medically necessary, treatment is typically covered by BlueCross BlueShield, Aetna, Cigna, UnitedHealthcare, Humana, Medicare, Medicaid, and Tricare. We verify your coverage at no cost before your appointment.

How many treatments will I need?

Most patients require 1–3 treatment sessions depending on the extent of vein disease. Your specialist will create a personalized plan during your free initial consultation including an ultrasound mapping of affected veins.

How soon will I see results?

Many patients notice symptom improvement within days. Visual results typically appear within 4–12 weeks as the treated veins are naturally absorbed by the body. Ultrasound confirmation is done at follow-up.

Is the procedure painful?

Local anesthesia is used for all procedures. Most patients report only mild pressure or warmth during treatment. Post-procedure soreness is typically minimal and resolves within a few days.
